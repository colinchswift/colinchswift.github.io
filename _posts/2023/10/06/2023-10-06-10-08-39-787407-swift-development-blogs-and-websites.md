## 1. Swift by Sundell
[Swift by Sundell](https://www.swiftbysundell.com/) is a popular blog by John Sundell, known for his in-depth articles, tips, and tricks related to Swift development. This blog covers a wide range of topics, from language features and app architecture to testing and tooling. With well-written tutorials and podcast episodes, Swift by Sundell is a valuable resource for Swift enthusiasts.

## 2. NSHipster
[NSHipster](https://nshipster.com/) is a blog created by Mattt Thompson that explores lesser-known and underused APIs in the Apple ecosystem. While not exclusively dedicated to Swift, NSHipster provides deep dives into various Swift topics, frameworks, and libraries. With its detailed guides and insightful articles, NSHipster is a must-read for iOS developers looking to expand their knowledge.

## 3. Ray Wenderlich
[Ray Wenderlich](https://www.raywenderlich.com/) is a widely recognized platform for iOS and Swift developers. It offers a vast array of tutorials, screencasts, and books covering not only Swift but also other essential development topics like SwiftUI, Combine, and more. With a large and active community of contributors, Ray Wenderlich is an excellent resource for both beginners and experienced developers alike.

## 4. Paul Hudson
[Paul Hudson](https://www.hackingwithswift.com/) runs an extensive Swift learning resource called Hacking with Swift. This platform provides a range of free and paid Swift tutorials, books, and video courses. Paul's engaging teaching style and in-depth tutorials make Hacking with Swift a valuable resource for learning Swift and iOS development.

## 5. Hacking with Swift
[Hacking with Swift](https://www.hackingwithswift.com/) is a website maintained by Paul Hudson, offering a plethora of Swift tutorials, projects, and challenges. The content is structured in a way that allows you to start from scratch and gradually move towards more advanced concepts. With its hands-on approach, Hacking with Swift is an excellent resource for practicing and reinforcing your Swift skills.

## 6. SwiftLee
[SwiftLee](https://www.avanderlee.com/) is a blog by Antoine van der Lee, where he shares his experiences and knowledge about Swift development. The articles cover various topics, including architectural patterns, coding techniques, and helpful tips. With its practical approach and insightful content, SwiftLee offers valuable advice for Swift developers of all levels.

## 7. Erica Sadun
[Erica Sadun](http://ericasadun.com/) is a well-known author and iOS developer. Her blog provides a mix of Swift tutorials, reference guides, and thought-provoking articles. Erica's expertise and contributions to the Swift community make her blog a go-to resource for those wanting to deepen their Swift knowledge.

## 8. Swift.org
[Swift.org](https://swift.org/) is the official website for the Swift programming language. It hosts a variety of resources, including documentation, forums, and the latest Swift releases. This website is an essential bookmark for staying up-to-date with the official Swift language updates and developments.

## 9. Swift Weekly
[Swift Weekly](https://swiftweekly.github.io/) is a curated newsletter that delivers the latest Swift news, articles, and community content directly to your inbox every week. It's a great way to stay informed about what's happening in the Swift community without having to scour the web for updates.

## 10. iOS Goodies
[iOS Goodies](https://ios-goodies.com/) is a weekly newsletter that covers various iOS development topics, including Swift, libraries, tools, and best practices. Each issue includes hand-picked articles, tutorials, and open-source projects, making it a valuable resource to discover new Swift content and stay up-to-date with the iOS development community.

## Conclusion
With the ever-evolving nature of Swift and iOS development, keeping up with the latest advancements and best practices is essential. The blogs and websites mentioned above are excellent resources for Swift developers to learn, grow, and stay informed about the latest trends and techniques in the Swift ecosystem. So, bookmark these sites, subscribe to newsletters, and make the most out of their valuable content!
